# Model Card — hotel-pvar-drivers-linreg

**Status:** Skeleton (Phase 1). Populated during Phase 4 implementation.

## Model details

- **Type:** OLS with HC3 robust SE (default) and cluster-robust SE on `property_id`
  (recommended headline). Quantile regression at τ ∈ {0.5, 0.75, 0.9} for tail-driver
  attribution. RLM (Huber's T) as an outlier-resilience check.
- **Inputs:** `log_lead_time`, weekend / holiday flags, cyclical month, `property_tier`,
  `channel`, `country`, `property_tier × channel` interaction, `log_expected_rate`,
  `star_rating_centered`.
- **Output:** Coefficient estimates with 95% CIs (HC3 + cluster + bootstrap) — used for
  attribution, not prediction.

## Intended use

- Attribution analysis: which factors drive OTA price variance?
- A/B test priors: feed coefficients into experimental-design power calculations.
- Operations targeting: identify supplier-tier × channel combinations that warrant
  connectivity QA investment.

## Out-of-scope use

- Forecasting future rates (this is an attribution model, not a forecast).
- Causal inference (the regression is observational; the appendix discusses what would
  be needed for IV / DiD / RDD).
- Use on real production data without re-fitting (the DGP is synthetic).

## Training data

Synthetic, generated by `src/pvar_linreg/dgp.py`. Default sample 200k rows; full DGP
~5.5M rows.

## Evaluation

Held-out 80/20 split. R² (in-sample, adjusted, out-of-sample 5-fold CV), RMSE, MAE.
Coefficient stability via 1000-replicate cluster bootstrap. Recovered-vs-true
coefficient plot.

## Ethical considerations

The data is synthetic — no privacy or licence concerns. Real-world deployment requires
re-fitting on partner-extranet data and addressing the within-property dependence
explicitly (mixed-effects model strongly preferred over OLS at that point).

## Caveats

- Synthetic data may be too clean. The injected ~1% decimal-point errors and channel
  noise mitigate this; the diagnostics validate the response is realistic.
- BP / White reject heteroskedasticity at this N regardless of structure. Read the
  residual plot, not the p-value.
- DW detects AR(1) but says nothing about higher-order dependence — within-property
  cluster-robust SE is the right move.
